About

When you picture a scientist who comes to mind? A male or female? Well, a study that applied the DAST method reported that students from kindergarten to high school perceive scientists as male. The children's drawings contained very few portrayals of female scientists and these few drawings were mostly drawn by female students. For example, in a study among students from kindergarten through fifth grade there were only 28 pictures of female scientists out of 4,807, and all of these 28 drawings were drawn by girls (Makarova et al.). With that being said, we decided to investigate the issue of how gender/gender roles affect educational career success for women in STEM. We all agree that this is a problem that many face in today's society. This issue has been affecting women around the world for decades so we think it is time to make a change. Another problem is that this issue can even affect salaries per gender, for certain jobs men can be paid more than women. Since we are starting the steps towards our career, it is important to know these things so that we can prepare ourselves for what's to come when we enter our field of choice, and when we take classes that lead to our profession. There are many organizations that work towards breaking this stigma and making the STEM field more open for women. For further information, see the organizations listed in the section below.

Sterotypes Relating to the Science Industry and the Impact on Young Women

Gender-science sterotyping has a negative imapct on young women in STEM related academics. This can inhibit them from wanting to pursue a degree in a "male dominant field" because they will be seen as having to prove themselves more than a young man would. According to "The Gender Gap in STEM Fields: The Impact of the Gender Stereotype of Math and Science on Secondary Students' Career Aspirations", "The study reported that math, physics and chemistry had the strongest connotation as masculine academic subjects. Moreover, it showed that science subjects were not only rated as masculine but also associated with a set of attributes commonly associated with masculinity such as being hard, complex, based on thinking rather than on feelings.”

Video Title

This video highlights how big the gender gap in STEM fields is. In computing, women make up only 25% of the workforce. In engineering careers, women only make up 12% of the workforce. It's not that women do not seek these careers. Instead, the problem is that women feel isolated and marginalized in these fields.

Mind the gender gap: the impact of university education on the entrepreneurial entry of female and male STEM graduates

Click here to read this PDF in full!


In this PDF article surrounding the gender gap, our authors Evila Piva and Paola Rovelli talk about the impact of university education on the entrepreneurial entry of female and male STEM graduates. Our authors start this article off by introducing us to STEM and propose a few reasons why they think women engage less in this field. They then talk about a few studies and list some very interesting information about these studies. Women often feel a lack of confidence and intimidation in fields that are predominately men. Piva and Rovelli also brought to light the peculiar challenges women face when trying to gain the same access men have. There are tons and tons of fascinating and significant statistics, facts, and side notes that the authors include in this article. Piva and Rovelli not only bring this issue to light and talk about the problems in it; they also provide solutions and some ways to go about improving it. One statistic; only 34.2% of STEM graduates are women (Piva and Rovelli, pg. 150).

STEM for her organization

“The STEM for her mission is to promote education to create awareness, excitement, and opportunities among girls and young women to launch successful STEM-related careers” (STEM for her). They offer many helpful experiences for women including field trips, professional speakers, virtual programs, curriculum development, and they even offer resources for clubs and groups to meet after school. They also make their organization accessible for those who may not be able to afford college tuition by offering scholarships for summer programs/college tuition. STEM for her has many corporate partnerships. Some of the partnerships are well known companies such as Audi, Nestle, Whole Foods, and multiple others that adds to the credibility of this organization.

The education gender gap and the demographic transition in developing countries

Click here to read this PDF in full!


The article linked above, “The education gender gap and the demographic transition in developing countries”, by Nguyen Thang Dao and colleagues, focuses on the relationship between the gender gap and economic growth in developing countries. This article sheds light on the positive impacts of decreasing the gender gap can have on a countries economy and the lives of women in general. The data supports calls to action to decrease the gender gap in education and the work place because of the outstanding benefits.
